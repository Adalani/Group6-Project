Soccer

Soccer, otherwise known as Futbol (football) is a sport where players work as a team, kicking a ball around, to try to score as many goals on the opponent's goalie as they can before the game ends.

Soccer is a great sport where players can:

  • Learn teamwork
  • Work on communication
  • Identify their strengths

More Details

A soccer team is comprised of 11 players, one of them being the goalkeeper.

The positions include:

  • Goalkeeper
    • The player that mans the net. They are the only ones that can touch the ball with their hands.
  • Defender
    • They are the team's last resort before the goalie. They play immediately in front of the goalie and are usually the players that are the most aware of where their hands are so as to not touch the ball with their hands, consequently giving the other team a free kick.
  • Midfielder
    • Just as the name suggests, they play in the middle between the defenders and the forwards. They serve to reinforce the team's structure, being able to both play offense and defense.
  • Forward
    • They are the masters of offense. These players are usually the ones responsible for all the goals that are scored in each match. Forwards usually possess qualities such as speed, mastery of ball movement and precise shooting.

Each player will have the opportunity to choose the position they would like to play. If the player does not have a desired position, the manager will assign them a position.
