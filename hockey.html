Hockey

Hockey is a sport where two teams, playing on a sheet of ice attempt to score as many goals on the other team's goalie as they can before time runs out.

Hockey can help improve:

  • Your balance (since you'll be on skates)
  • Your endurance
  • Your communication skills

A hockey team is made up of about 11 players, one of them being a goalie. Only 5 players per team are allowed on the ice at once.

The positions include:

  • Goalkeeper
    • The player in charge of protecting the cage. They wear the most equipment on the team, especially a full facial mask to protect themselves from oncoming shots.
  • Defenders
    • The last line of defnse before the goalie. There are two defenders per team on the ice, at a time. They are in charge of blocking the majority of the shots, repelling oncoming opponents, and just overall protecting the goalie.
  • Forwards
    • Snipers, fast hands, quick feet - they are the goalie's worst nightmare. Forwards are offensive specialists, usually responsible for the majority of the goals scored by both teams.
      • Forward positions include:
        • Left Wing
        • Center
        • Right Wing

    Games will last a total of 60 minutes, divided into 3 periods, lasting 20 minutes each. There will be a 3-minute break in between each period.

    Each player will have the opportunity to choose the position they would like to play. If the player does not have a desired position, the manager will assign them a position.
